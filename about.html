About

Commercial photography with a real-world center of gravity.

I’m Michael Mejia, a commercial photographer working across sports, fashion, people, process, and curated reality. My strongest images come from real environments, real texture, and subjects who feel like themselves, not placeholders inside a concept.

The work is cinematic and editorial, but it stays believable. That balance matters to me. I want the photograph to feel elevated without feeling fake, controlled without feeling stiff, and sharp enough to serve the brief without losing its pulse.

Curated reality is the phrase I use for that approach. It is not documentary purity and it is not overproduced fiction. It is a disciplined way of shaping what is already true so the image lands harder.

Available for commissions across cities, with a working rhythm that adapts well to agency teams, brand clients, editorial assignments, and location-based production.

How I Work

Clear point of view. Clean execution.

Commercially useful

The image has to do its job. It needs presence, structure, and enough clarity to live across campaigns, editorial placements, decks, and brand systems.

Editorially credible

I care about taste, restraint, pacing, and what the frame includes or leaves out. The work should feel considered, not assembled from generic photography habits.

Rooted in reality

Real people, real process, real movement, real environments. That is usually where the image gets its authority and where the assignment becomes harder to fake.

Availability
